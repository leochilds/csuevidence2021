Brennan Lee Mulligan Tribute

Brennan Lee Mulligan is a Writer, Actor, and Comedian from New York. He is also a professional Game Master, for Dropout. He is known for his work on Dimension 20, a Dungeons and Dragons actual-play web series, and the webcomic Strong Female Protagonist.

Born on the 4th of January, 1988 as the son of Elaine Lee, Brennan Lee Mulligan was introduced to Dungeons & Dragons by his mother at age nine, first gamemastering at age ten. In November 2015, Mulligan was a contestant on the American version of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire and won US$50,000 (equivalent to $54,591 in 2020) after incorrectly answering the $100,000-question. By 2020, he was an alumnus of SUNY Ulster (having majored in philosophy/humanities) and lived in Los Angeles. On January 21, 2021, Mulligan announced his engagement to his fiancée—Izzy Roland—via Instagram.

For many years, Brennan was the lead writer for the LARPing summer camp, The Wayfinder Experience. He also authored Strong Female Protagonist, a webcomic he created with artist and writer Molly Ostertag. The first pages were published in 2012 and it went on hiatus in 2018, as both creators were too busy to continue the project. SFP acquired a large following over the years, and many still eagerly await the final chapter, whenever it may come.

Brennan performed and taught comedy at the Upright Citizen's Brigade in his twenties, leading to his employment at CollegeHumor in 2017. His first appearance was in the sketch Who's the Real Cop?, where he played a police superintendent. He has continued to write and perform in sketches for CollegeHumor since. His sketches include, Defender of the Basic, You're Too Sad to Argue With, and the Message From Your Favorite CEO series.

In 2016, Brennan starred in the short film Bev, a comedic twist on trauma and group therapy, co-directed by Samy Burch and Alex Machanik. On the 17th of January, 2017, Fallen for You was released. It was co-written by and starred Brennan Lee Mulligan and Ashley Skidmore, following Ashley as she fell in love with, and subsequently ruined the life of, her guardian angel, Todd.

Brennan Lee Mulligan is writer, executive producer, and game master of CollegeHumor's Dimension 20. The actual-play webseries has run three core campaigns (Fantasy High, The Unsleeping City, and A Crown of Candy), and several more Side Quests, including Tiny Heist, Of Mice and Murder, and upcoming season The Seven, which follow a group of NPCs from Fantasy High. It is highly acclaimed for both its creative settings, incredible acting, and relatively short structure. Unlike other actual-play shows, such as Critical Role and The Adventure Zone, D20's longest seasons only span twenty episodes, making them a much easier introduction to table top games.
